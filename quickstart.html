Quick Start Guide

Create your first 3D terrain in just a few minutes

Getting Started

You'll see a grid where you can select your terrain size:

Sculpting Your Terrain

Basic Controls

Move around your scene using Noggit-style camera controls:

  • Right Mouse Button + drag to look around
  • WASD keys to move forward, left, back, right
  • Q/E keys to move down/up
  • Shift to move faster

Sculpting Tools

The terrain editing tools are in the right sidebar. Click "Sculpt" to expand the sculpting panel.

Raise and Lower:

  • Left-click on terrain to raise it
  • Right-click to lower it
  • Adjust brush size and strength with the sliders
  • Hold and drag to paint continuously

Smooth:

  • Switch to Smooth mode to blend rough areas
  • Great for creating natural-looking hills and valleys

Flatten:

  • Creates perfectly flat surfaces
  • Useful for building platforms or roads

Adding Textures

Opening the Content Browser

Press Ctrl+Space to open the Content Browser: your asset library.

Painting Textures

  1. Click the "Textures" tab in the Content Browser
  2. Click on any texture (like "Grass" or "Dirt") to select it
  3. The texture paint tool activates automatically
  4. Left-click and drag on your terrain to paint
  5. Switch textures and layer them for variety

Painting works like Photoshop: Each texture blends naturally with others, so you can create realistic transitions between grass, dirt, stone, and more.

Placing 3D Objects

Models and Decorations

Still in the Content Browser (Ctrl+Space), switch to the "3D Models" tab.

  1. Click on any model (trees, rocks, buildings)
  2. A ghost preview appears. This shows where your object will be placed
  3. Move your mouse to position it
  4. Left-click to place the object
  5. Right-click or press ESC to cancel

Once placed, click any object to select it. You'll see gizmos (colored arrows and circles) that let you:

  • Move it (drag the arrows)
  • Rotate it (drag the circles)
  • Scale it (drag the boxes)

Adding 2D Sprites

Want to add trees or decorative elements that always face the camera?

  1. Open Content Browser (Ctrl+Space)
  2. Go to the "Sprites" tab
  3. Select a sprite (trees, plants, effects)
  4. Place it just like 3D models

Sprites are perfect for vegetation and particle-like effects. They automatically rotate to face you, creating a convincing 3D effect.

Decals and Details

Adding Decals

Decals let you project images onto your terrain: perfect for paths, cracks, or detail textures.

  1. Open the Decal panel in the right sidebar
  2. Click "Enable Decal Mode"
  3. Pick a decal from the library
  4. Click on your terrain to project it
  5. Adjust size and rotation in the properties panel
Tip:

Use decals sparingly for the best visual impact!

Atmosphere and Environment

Setting the Mood

Expand the Skybox and Fog panels to create atmosphere:

Skybox:

  • Choose time of day: Dawn, Noon, Dusk, or Midnight
  • Adjust size and blur for dramatic skies

Fog:

  • Select fog mode: None, Atmospheric, or Thick
  • Adjust intensity with the slider
  • Change fog color to match your scene

Tips for Success

  • Start Small: Begin with a 2Ã—2 terrain to learn the tools, then scale up to larger projects.
  • Layer Textures: Don't use just one texture. Blend 3-4 different textures for realistic, natural-looking ground.
  • Use Reference: Look at real-world photos for inspiration on terrain shape and texture placement.
  • Save Often: While auto-save has your back, get in the habit of saving manually after major changes.
  • Experiment: The best way to learn is to play! Try different combinations of objects, textures, and lighting.

Common Questions

Q: How do I delete an object?
A: Click to select it, then press Delete or use the Object Properties panel.
Q: Can I import my own textures?
A: Yes! Save your project first, then use the "Import" button in the Content Browser.
Q: Why can't I see my custom textures?
A: Custom textures appear after you've saved your project at least once.
Q: How do I make terrain taller?
A: Increase the brush strength slider and hold left-click longer, or click repeatedly in the same spot.
Q: The terrain looks blocky, how do I fix it?
A: Use the Smooth tool to blend sharp edges and create natural transitions.

What's Next?

  • Display Cases: Showcase 3D models in glass cases
  • Particle Zones: Add ambient effects like fireflies or falling leaves
  • Vertex Painting: Fine-tune terrain colors for extra detail
